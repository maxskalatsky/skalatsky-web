Cooper and Hunter

Company Snapshot
Cooper and Hunter is an HVAC manufacturer and distributor, cooperandhunter.us, selling through a Pro Tech dealer network and retail channels including Home Depot and Amazon. The ask on this call: the CFO wants to grow sales aggressively.
The Digital Signal
Hook Missing Fit Partial Relevance Partial
SEO grade A. Visibility, first impressions, and content structure all score strong. Authority is the one weak mark, a thin link profile against the sites currently outranking cooperandhunter.us. Agent readiness lands at Capable, meaning AI assistants can already read and surface the site, but that thin authority is why better known competitors get cited first.
Cooperandhunter.us Senville
Hook Missing Missing

SEO Grade A A

Cooper and Hunter is already ahead of this direct competitor on positioning fundamentals, SEO, and AI visibility. The open question for the CFO: do they stay ahead as Senville closes the gap, or does Senville's stronger trust signal start winning the comparison that AI assistants and buyers both run.
Findings
What is working
1.SEO fundamentals score an A. Indexing, page speed, and content structure already do what search engines and AI assistants reward.
2.Agent readiness sits at Capable, ahead of Basic or Emerging. The site is already legible to AI assistants and can appear in their answers.
3.Real third party proof and real spend already exist: a 30+ project gallery, sponsorships with NASCAR, the Yankees, and the Florida Panthers, 12.5K Instagram followers with an active posting cadence, a named Pro Tech dealer program, and a new 100,000 square foot distribution center built to serve Central US growth.
4.20+ years in business and products sold in 50+ countries give real scale to point to, credibility a newer brand cannot claim.
What is missing
1.Brand hook is missing entirely per the Digital Signal. A visitor cannot tell what the company does or why it matters without digging.
2.Authority is the one weak SEO mark, thin link profile compared to the sites currently outranking cooperandhunter.us.
3.No social proof number appears near the top of the site, while Senville leads its own homepage with "Installed in Over 500,000 Homes."
4.Retail review presence is thin and mixed. Home Depot listings show low volume, 2.0 out of 5 on 7 reviews for one mini split SKU, 3.0 out of 5 on 2 reviews for another, a sign review generation is not being actively managed at the point of sale.
AI Recommendation Layer
Confirmed, live ChatGPT test
Two queries run live. First, "what is the best HVAC mini split brand for a dealer or installer to carry," scored on reliability, dealer support, parts availability, and profit potential. Cooper and Hunter does not appear. The full ranked table names Mitsubishi Electric, Daikin, Fujitsu, Gree, LG, and Samsung. Second, "which mini split brand should I buy for my home." Cooper and Hunter does not appear there either. Best overall goes to Mitsubishi Electric, best value to Daikin, best budget to Gree, with Fujitsu, LG, and Samsung also named.
This is the sharpest finding in the whole diagnostic. Cooper and Hunter is absent at both decision points that drive its sales, the dealer who decides what to stock and the homeowner who decides what to buy. Not ranked low, not mentioned at all, while six named competitors fill every slot. This is the visual to lead with in the room.
Decision Maker
Fractional CFO, newly hired
Founder and CEO is Marat Terchiyev, publicly quoted on the Dallas area expansion and framed around reaching more of the country faster, a growth and logistics mindset. The CFO on this call is fractional and was just brought on, which is why nothing surfaced in public search, LinkedIn has not caught up yet.
A newly hired fractional CFO is usually brought in specifically to professionalize growth and spend decisions, and works across multiple companies at once, so the calibration is efficiency and numbers first, not brand story. Lead with the spend already committed not converting frame. That is the argument a fractional CFO is paid to act on fast, and this diagnostic is likely surfacing gaps in Cooper and Hunter's marketing return that no one inside the company has laid out this plainly yet.
The Frame For This Call
This is not a spend problem
Cooper and Hunter is already paying for brand: three major sports sponsorships, an active social channel with real followers and real project content, a new distribution center built for growth. None of that spend shows up where a buyer or an AI assistant actually looks first, the website's hook, its trust signals, and its authority. That is the sales problem in one line: the top of the funnel is funded, the conversion layer is not capturing it. For a CFO focused on aggressive growth, the fastest path is not more spend, it is making the spend already committed actually convert.
The Sales Opportunity, Lightest To Heaviest
1
Convert visitors already on the site into leads
Days, no new spend
  • Move the project gallery above the fold, where a buyer or dealer is deciding whether to inquire
  • Add one proof number to the homepage headline so trust is established before the visitor has to dig
  • Put existing Instagram project photos to work as sales proof on the site, not just social content
2
Turn buyers and dealers into repeat sales and referrals
Days, low spend
  • A post purchase review flow so satisfied homeowners generate the proof retail listings are missing
  • Sales enablement material the Pro Tech network can use to close new dealers and larger orders
  • Short interviews with top dealers and repeat homeowners to capture the actual reasons they buy and stay
3
Win the moment a dealer or homeowner is choosing between brands
Scoped project
  • Comparison content built from real dealer language, aimed at the decision to carry Cooper and Hunter over Daikin or Gree
  • The same comparison built for the homeowner decision between those brands
  • Get named in the answer when a dealer or homeowner asks an AI assistant which brand to buy or carry
4
Grow the number of dealers selling the product
Ongoing
  • A dealer recruitment push with real media spend into the new Lancaster, Texas territory, turning new distribution capacity into new accounts
  • Ongoing press and dealer network placements that put Cooper and Hunter in front of installers who have not yet been asked to carry it
Walk In With

The pitch that follows is about converting spend already committed into sales, not asking for new budget.
